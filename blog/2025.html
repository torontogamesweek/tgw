2025 Toronto Games Week Recap

2025 was the biggest year yet for TGW, with over 4500 combined attendees at 35+ events over seven magical days.



At Toronto Games Week 2025, you could:

  • Connect with queer joy and rage in DEI or DIE Variety Show!
  • Scour High Park for fungi and butterflies in scavenger hunts!
  • Wheel down dark forest paths to the Night Parkcade!
  • Level up your industry knowledge and contacts!
  • Play massive videogames projected on concrete silos at the waterfront!

Special guests

  • Sherwin Tjia (You Are a Cat, Strip Spelling Bee), choose-your-own-adventure creator and quirky event organizer from Montreal who brought the contemplative SUNCHASER flight simulator experience!
  • Paolo Pedercini (Every Day the Same Dream, New York Times Simulator), political game designer and DIY curator from Pittsburgh!
  • Terry Cavanagh (Super Hexagon, VVVVVV), wildly prolific game designer and IndieCade award winner from Ireland!

Thursday, June 12: Kicking Off in Style

We kicked off Toronto Games Week with Giant Video Games at the waterfront. We had massive playable projections lighting up the historic Canada Malting Silos.

Players were selected through a contest involving giant golden tickets.

Mayor Olivia Chow joined in on the fun!

(photos: Yevi Kaliuzhka)

XP Game Summit brought hundreds of professionals together for insights and connections across two days. Behaviour Interactive hosted a meet & greet at their new Toronto office.

(photo: Ryan of XP Summit)

At InterAccess, visitors could experience xXx_FANTASYLAND_xXx a transgressive multimedia project combining videogames, live performance, and projection mapping to explore identity and representation.

(photo: Liam Mackenzie)

Tanya Kan from Vivid Foundry hosted Journaling for Game Makers at Fort York Library, and Mathew Iantorno introduced Spellbinder, a tabletop game that turns books into spellcasting components through Making + Playing a Little Free Library RPG

Friday, June 13: Community and Connection

Friday brought the beloved DEI or DIE Variety Showcase & Queer Social to TMU's eighth floor, where hosts Gav Sarafian and Hannah Beatty connected the community through performances, conversations, and gameplay that celebrated queer joy and rage.

(photo: jcallejasrodriguez)

The G.R.I.D. provided structured playtesting and feedback to delighted devs at George Brown College.

(photo: Mega Power Games)

Saturday, June 14: Adventure Central

Saturday was packed with outdoor adventures and creative activities. The Hunt for the Brightwing Butterfly led participants through High Park's winding paths on an immersive scavenger hunt, while the Rat Race | Adventure in the P.A.T.H. challenged teams to conquer North America's largest underground pedestrian network.

(photo: jesse of lockwoodimmersive)

In Search of the Godmama offered an alternative ritual for exchanging goddesses from different cultures, and Haggle on the Grange turned the park into a large-scale negotiation game.


The beloved Kitten Cup Tea Party returned to Grange Park with a capybara theme, complete with tea-posing traditions and indie game demos.

(photo: jsajm quino)

We had a wealth of Game Jam Day events, with Board Game and PICO-8 MicroJams, featuring workshops by Andrew Tran and Adam Saltsman from Finji, followed by "What's Next, Jammer?", a discussion about the cultural and economic value of game jams.


The Pixel Power Play showcase at Red Bull/Overactive Media gave attendees early access to upcoming games, while Vector Festival's Game Residency Keynote at InterAccess featured documentarian Min Sook Lee discussing autofiction in videogames.


We closed the night with the mysterious Night Bike Parkcade, a pop-up arcade with a two-wheeled theme featuring bike-related videogames, protest games, and secret clues to find us.

Sunday, June 15: Making and Playing


Sunday opened with hands-on creation at Games Made Open at TTL Makerspace, where participants could hack with open-source tools like Godot and create their own Gameboy cartridges. The Board Game Design Jam at Snakes & Lattes College invited teams to prototype boardgames in a day.


High Park hosted Metazooa Live!, an evolution-themed scavenger hunt using phones to track plants and animals while building a tree of life.


The Out of Bounds 2ronto speedrunning event at Society Clubhouse showcased women and femme players demonstrating incredible gaming skills throughout the weekend.


At Hideaway Studios, we had Dream Game Incorporated, where participants' drawings came to life in a playable game prototype with live musical accompaniment. The Indie Game Emporium at Houndstooth offered a chance to meet local developers and purchase zines, diy games, and other game-art objects.


The day wrapped with experimental performances at Play House at See-Scape, with live electronic music and games, and then the NextGen Showcase at CSI Spadina, featuring curated games from Toronto's emerging talent.

Monday, June 16: Building Community

On Monday we had Deck Building at Lillian H. Smith Library where participants created trading cards, and The Playful Page conversation at Palmerston Library featuring Holly Gramazio and Jim Munroe discussing the intersection of writing and games.


Team Up Activated brought Marvel Rivals tournaments to Invictus Game Station, while The Sound of Soft Rains' New Sci-Fi Game at the Canadian Music Centre offered a behind-the-scenes look at video game music creation.


The IGDA Game Programmers Roundtable at George Brown College brought together veteran programmers to share career insights and advice.


Monday ended with the joyful Parachute Club in Christie Pits Park, where people of all ages came together for playful community activities with a giant rainbow parachute.

(photo: kreztakingphotos)

Tuesday, June 17: Art and Preservation


Tuesday started with Sherwin Tjia's SUNCHASER at InterAccess, where participants took turns flying a flight simulator while chasing an endless sunset projected on the walls.


The Portable Preservation showcase at the Royal Ontario Museum celebrated handheld gaming history with Game Boy cameras, rare accessories, and hidden gems from gaming's past.


We gathered for microtalks at Bonus Stage at TacoTaco, and then onwards to Seriously Fun at Danu Social House, dedicated to games for change, educational games, and serious game development.

Wednesday, June 18

We wrapped it all up with the Toronto Games Week Closing Party at Trinity Square Video with OMNIVOX, a magical cube with 4,096 LEDs and a custom playful experience designed by Callum Hay, Sara Vinten, and Jessica Mak.
