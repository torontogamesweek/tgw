TGW Press Release 2026

TORONTO, ON — Toronto Games Week (TGW) returns June 11–17, 2026, transforming the city into a vibrant playground of videogames, tabletop experiences, interactive art, live performances, workshops, community gatherings, and competitive gaming. Featuring independently organized events hosted throughout Toronto, TGW continues its mission of celebrating games as culture, creativity, and connection.

Presented through a collaborative city-wide model, Toronto Games Week brings together developers, artists, community organizers, performers, and players of all kinds for seven days of inclusive and experimental programming.

"With 50+ event organizers, we're transforming parks, silos, bars, parking lots, and underground spaces into pop-up arcades and playful takeovers. Chase us through the PATH, play with a parachute in the park, and follow clues to cooperative games in a secret location after dark. Toronto is a playground. You should join us!" — Marie LeBlanc Flanagan, co-founder of Toronto Games Week

Following an incredible TGW 2025 that welcomed more than 4,200 attendees, Toronto Games Week returns with more unforgettable events.

Highlight Events

  • Giant Video Games & TGW Kickoff — a waterfront celebration featuring massive playable game projections on the historic Canada Malting Silos.
  • Hand Eye Society x Luminato — Torontron arcade cabinets featuring indie games by local developers and artists are coming to Luminato at Sankofa Square.
  • Rocket League Cup 2026 — a soccer-adjacent video game tournament featuring high-flying cars and teamplay.
  • Out of Bounds Torontrois — Lady Arcaders' third annual speedrunning event featuring two days of exceptional gameplay and programming.
  • NextGen Showcase — a curated exhibition spotlighting emerging game creators from Toronto's game development scene with Jenny Windom.
  • The Beautiful Game — a playable history of soccer video games spanning 50 years of gaming history.
  • Pokémon Proxy Party — a laid-back art anthology for Pokémon fans and creators to contribute to a community-built collection of 151 custom Pokémon designs.
  • Live Video Game Orchestra — Join a twelve piece jazz orchestra for classic video music from Pokémon, Zelda, Undertale and more.
  • TGW Closing Party — the event series finale with help from Interactive Ontario featuring performances, music, socializing, and interactive installations.

Special Guests

You also have the chance to spot three special guests throughout the city during the event. Julián Cordero, creator of the acclaimed hand-drawn game Despelote, will appear at Stoppage Time to play the game live. Jenny Windom, managing partner at Wholesome Games, will talk at Bonus Stage as well as highlight emerging creators at NextGen Showcase. Joining them is Eric Zimmerman, co-author of Rules of Play and founding faculty member of the NYU Game Center in New York City. Eric will be at the opening night kickoff as well as the Night Parkcade.

Additional Programming

  • Experimental performances and playable installations

Community Drives Toronto Games Week

Toronto Games Week's decentralized format encourages participation from organizations and creators across the city, with events taking place in libraries, parks, galleries, maker spaces, community venues, cultural hubs, and independent businesses.

The event series remains committed to accessibility, inclusivity, and community-driven participation, with most events offered free of charge or pay-what-you-can.

TGW continues to demonstrate the rapid growth of gaming from niche hobby to universal language of play. From family-friendly activities like the Pokémon Proxy Party to competitive gaming at the Rocket League Cup 2026, Toronto Games Week has something for everyone.

About Toronto Games Week

Toronto Games Week is a collective coordination of events organized independently by dozens of organizations, curators, companies, creators, and communities. Coordinated by Marie LeBlanc Flanagan and Jim Munroe, TGW demonstrates that when communities organize around shared joy, the results exceed anything corporate planning could achieve.
